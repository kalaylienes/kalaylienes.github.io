    IARVR 06 - Conclusion - TUDa 25/26

    Kalaylienes
    March 15, 2026

    This project explored the design and implementation of a custom locomotion technique and an indirect object interaction system in a VR environment.

    The locomotion system was based on the idea of controlling a horse through a carrot on a stick metaphor rather than through direct input such as a joystick. While this approach introduced several technical challenges, it ultimately resulted in a playful and understandable locomotion mechanic where movement emerges from the relationship between multiple objects. The interaction phase complemented this idea by introducing a hook-based manipulation system combined with a shooting mechanic for object rotation.

    Throughout development, many small challenges had to be addressed, ranging from animation synchronization issues to physics stability problems and interaction design adjustments. Solving these issues required iterative testing and practical workarounds rather than a single large technical solution. In the end, the final system successfully demonstrates both a custom locomotion technique and a simple interaction puzzle that can be completed within the VR environment.

    Beyond the technical implementation, this project also provided valuable personal experience. Building interactive systems is always an enjoyable part of software development, but developing something directly playable in a virtual environment made the process particularly engaging and challenging. Being able to repeatedly test small changes and immediately experience their effect inside the VR world was both motivating and rewarding.

    Within my overall studies, this course became one of the most memorable and enjoyable experiences. Even though I invested a significant amount of time into the project, I genuinely enjoyed every minute of the development process. Watching the system gradually evolve into a working and playable experience was extremely satisfying and made the effort feel worthwhile.

    Finally, I would like to sincerely thank Prof. Dr. Jan Gugenheimer for organizing the course in such a creative and engaging way and for giving us the opportunity to explore VR development through a hands-on project. I am also very grateful to Ms. Yara Hartenstein for her continuous support, her patience, and her always friendly and helpful guidance throughout the course. Their encouragement and support played a big role in making this learning experience both enjoyable and inspiring.
